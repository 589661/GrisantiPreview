Questions clients ask us first.

I just got arrested for DWI in New Jersey. What's the first thing I should do?

Don't assume the case is closed because you took the breath test or believe you failed it. New Jersey DWI cases turn on procedure: how the stop was conducted, whether field sobriety tests were administered correctly, whether the Alcotest device was calibrated and operated according to State Police protocol, whether the 20-minute observation period was honored, and whether the arresting officer's certifications were current. Each of these is a potential defense. Call us before your first court date and we'll review the discovery line by line.

Do I really need an attorney for a New Jersey home purchase?

New Jersey is one of the few states where attorney review is built into the standard real estate contract. For the three business days following execution, your attorney can modify or cancel the contract — and that window is where most problems get prevented. Beyond review, an attorney handles title issues, the closing disclosure, the deed, the closing itself, and any disputes that arise after. You get a single point of contact who is legally on your side, not the buyer's or seller's.

Why do I need a will if I don't have a lot of assets?

A will isn't just about distributing money. It names a guardian for minor children, designates an executor, prevents your estate from being distributed under New Jersey's intestacy statute, and spares your family the cost and delay of probate complications. Pairing a will with a power of attorney and a living will (advance directive) gives your loved ones clear authority if you can't speak for yourself.

What does "no fee unless we recover" actually mean?

For personal injury matters we accept on contingency, you do not pay legal fees out of pocket. If we do not recover compensation for you, you owe no attorney's fee. If we do, our fee is a percentage of the recovery, governed by New Jersey court rules. We explain the exact arrangement before you sign anything — and the initial consultation costs you nothing either way.
